Our Story

137 Years of Mastery

In 1887, master watchmaker Édouard Chronos set up his first atelier on the shores of Lake Geneva. His vision was singular: to create timepieces that would transcend generations, carrying within their mechanisms the soul of their maker.

Today, over a century later, each Chronos watch is still assembled by hand in our Geneva workshops, where 48 master watchmakers spend up to 600 hours crafting a single grand complication.

We do not make watches for the masses. We create heirlooms for the discerning few who understand that the finest things in life require patience, skill, and an unyielding pursuit of perfection.

Timeline

A Legacy of Innovation

1887
The First Atelier
Édouard Chronos opens his workshop in Geneva, producing 12 pocket watches in the first year. Each is sold before completion.
1923
The First Wristwatch
Responding to the changing tide, Chronos releases the Calibre I — its first wristwatch. It sells out in 3 days at the Basel Fair.
1965
The Tourbillon Heritage
Our most complex movement to date, the CH-65 Tourbillon, wins the Grand Prix de l'Horlogerie in Geneva, cementing our legacy.
2001
Entering the Digital Age
Chronos becomes the first luxury watchmaker to offer online pre-orders, while maintaining every traditional element of craftsmanship.
2024
Chronos India
We open our first South Asian flagship in Mumbai, bringing the Geneva experience to India's growing community of watch connoisseurs.
